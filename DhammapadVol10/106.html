THE FOOL IS HIS OWN ENEMY. SEEKING WEALTH, HE DESTROYS HIMSELF. By
"wealth" Buddha means everything that is outside you: power, prestige, money, sex --
anything that is outside you. The fool is extrovert; he never looks in. He accumulates
everything on the outside. His whole life is devoted to money, power, prestige, and
then one day death comes, but then it is too late. When death comes, he realizes that all
that he has been doing has been simply stupid because all is slipping out of his fingers.
All that he has been doing was making sandcastles. Just a blow of death, and
everything disappears like a dream.
Buddha says: SEEKING WEALTH, HE DESTROYS HIMSELF. He remains constantly
extrovert; hence he never becomes aware who he is, why he is, from where he comes, to
where he is going, what is his destiny, what is his significance, why this existence needs
him, what purpose he is supposed to fulfill, what fragrance has to be released by him.
