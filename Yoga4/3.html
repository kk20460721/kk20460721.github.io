Why am I saying this before I start talking about Patanjali? I am saying it because
up to now, Patanjali has always been commented upon by masochists. But
whatsoever I am going to say about Patanjali is going to be totally different from
all the other commentaries. I am not a masochist, I am not a sadist. I am
celebrating myself and I would like you to participate with me. My commentary
on Patanjali is going to be basically different from all the previous commentaries.
My commentary will be just the same as if Patanjali himself were commenting.
He was neither a sadist nor a masochist. He was a perfectly integrated man with
no inner illness, with no psychological problems, with no obsessions. He was
healthy, whole, integrated. Whatsoever he has said can be interpreted in three
ways. A sadist may come upon it, but that is rare because sadists are not

Stalin being interested in Patanjali, no. Sadists are not interested, so they have
not commented. Masochists are interested in religion, and they have commented
