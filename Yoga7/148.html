It happened once:
A terrified young woman patient went to the dentist and sat in the waiting room.
She had her sister with her to look after the three-month-old baby. Soon it was
her turn to go into the torture chamber.
As she sat in the operating chair, she said nervously to the dentist,"l don't know
which is the worst -- to have a tooth taken or to have a baby."
The dentist said, curtly, "Well, make your mind up quickly please. I have got a
lot of other people waiting."
And I would like to say that to Nisha also. She has been hanging around here.
Make up your mind. Decide. Just hanging around is not good. Either be here or
be somewhere else, but be. If you want to be here then be here, but then be
totally here. Then this place becomes your whole world and this moment
becomes your whole eternity. Or don't be here, but don't go on hanging. Be
somewhere else, that too is good. Then be there. It is not a question of where you
are. The question is, "Are you there?" Don't remain divided. Don't go on moving
in all directions: otherwise you will go mad.
