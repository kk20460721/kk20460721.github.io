KNOWLEDGE IS indirect, knowing is direct. Knowledge is through many mediums; it is not reliable.
Knowing is immediate, without any medium. Only knowing can be reliable.
This distinction has to be remembered. Knowledge is like a messenger comes and tells something
to you: the messenger may have misunderstood the message; the messenger may have added
something of his own into the message; the messenger may have dropped something from the
message; the messenger may have forgotten something from the message; the messenger may
have added his own interpretations into it, or the messenger may be simply cunning and deceptive.
And you have to rely on the messenger. You don’t have any direct approach to the source of the
message – this is knowledge.
