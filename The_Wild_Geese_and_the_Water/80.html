7
Life’s Complementariness
17 February 1981 am in Buddha Hall
The ﬁrst question:
Question 1
OSHO,
YOU OFTEN ADVISE US TO FOLLOW TWO COURSES OF ACTION THAT SEEM TO BE
INCOMPATIBLE WITH EACH OTHER. FIRST YOU TELL US TO BE COMPLETELY IN THE
MOMENT, WITHOUT MIND, TO BE OUR FEAR, OUR JOY, OR RAGE. THEN YOU TELL US TO
BECOME DETACHED FROM OUR EGOS, TO WATCH WHAT COMES UP WITHIN OURSELVES.
HOW CAN WE DO BOTH? PLEASE EXPLAIN.
LISA CHRISTENSON,
LOGIC IS NOT NECESSARILY EXISTENTIAL; it is intellectual. But existence has no obligation...
you see? Existence goes on its own way! It is not concerned with your question, it is not concerned
with my answer. It is absolutely illogical.
Logic will see contradiction. If logic was all, then certainly there was difﬁculty. But logic is not all,
just the way of the mind of looking at things.
Mind divides in polar opposites; the division is not
there in existence itself. Existence is undivided. The day and the night are one, the birth and death
