CHAPTER 5.
And I am here to change everything! Good!
[A sannyasin leaving for the West, asks Osho if the names he gives sannyasins are an indication of
how they were or what they can become
....
]
The name can say many things, and I give names to different people for different reasons. I have
given it to you as your goal. It means divine homelessness. And homelessness, the space we call
homelessness, is a space where you don’t belong to anybody and nobody belongs to you. It is utter
unattachment.
Ordinarily we are all seeking homes – home means security, safety. Homelessness means insecurity
– and when you are really happy in being homeless, your happiness cannot be destroyed. Nobody
can take it away, because it is not caused by anything.
When one is really homeless, one lives moment to moment. Then one is not planning for the future,
one cannot plan for the future. So all this is implied in homelessness.
It is the ultimate goal. That’s what buddha calls ’nirvana’ – an absolute space with no centre at all, a
centreless space.
