Devaprem, Camus’ book, MAN IN REVOLT has many great insights in it, but he still remains a
philosopher. He preaches, but he does not practice. You are asking me that in this book, MAN IN
REVOLT, ”he comes to the conclusion that all attempts to abolish injustice through revolutions are
bound to fail.” That’s a great insight.
It seems there is something intrinsic in the very mechanism of revolution that makes it bound to fail.
First, the revolutionary is created by the old society against which he is revolting; his values, his
ideals are not much different from the old. The only difference for him is that the right people are not
in power; otherwise, everything is right. Only the right people have to be in power, the wrong people
have to be removed, and the revolution will be accomplished in all dimensions of life.
