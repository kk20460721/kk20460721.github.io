Something is missing; he is prejudiced. He has joined a party.
A man of true understanding remains alone; he does not join any party, any
organization, any church, any religion. He is available to every form of
understanding, but he remains impartial. To me this impartiality is one of the
fundamentals of religion.
A Christian is not religious just because he is a Christian. A Hindu is not
religious just because he has become part of an organized doctrine. A Jaina is not
religious because he has chosen a certain party line.
An authentically religious man is individual.
He is alone, and in his aloneness there is great beauty, great splendor.
I teach you that aloneness. I teach you the beauty, and the grandeur, and the
fragrance of aloneness.
In your aloneness you will reach to the heights of Everest. In your aloneness you
will be able to touch the farthest star. In your aloneness you will blossom to your
total potential.
Never become a believer, never become a follower, never become a part of any
organization. Remain authentically true to yourself. Don't betray yourself.
