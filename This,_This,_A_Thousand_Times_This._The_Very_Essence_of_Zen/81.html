CHAPTER
11
No beginning and no end
6 June 1988 pm in Gautam the Buddha Auditorium
BELOVED OSHO,
A MONK CAME FROM JOSHU OSHO’S ASSEMBLY TO UKYU, WHO SAID TO HIM, ”WHAT DO
YOU FIND IN JOSHU’S TEACHING? IS THERE ANYTHING DIFFERENT FROM WHAT YOU FIND
HERE?”
THE MONK SAID, ”NOTHING DIFFERENT.”
UKYU SAID, ”IF THERE IS NOTHING DIFFERENT, WHY DON’T YOU GO BACK THERE?” AND
HE HIT HIM WITH HIS STICK.
THE MONK SAID, ”IF YOUR STICK HAD EYES TO SEE, YOU WOULD NOT STRIKE ME LIKE
THAT.”
UKYU SAID, ”TODAY I HAVE COME ACROSS A MONK,” AND HE GAVE HIM THREE MORE
BLOWS.
THE MONK WENT OUT. UKYU CALLED AFTER HIM AND SAID, ”ONE MAY RECEIVE UNFAIR
BLOWS.”
THE MONK TURNED BACK AND SAID, ”TO MY REGRET, THE STICK IS IN YOUR HAND.”
UKYU SAID, ”IF YOU NEED IT, I WILL LET YOU HAVE IT.”
