CHAPTER 9. SAVE ME FROM THE PILLAR!
To me, that is true religion: to be able to celebrate life is religion. In that very celebration you come
close to God. If one is able to celebrate, God is not far away; if one is not able to celebrate life, then
God does not exist for him. God appears only in deep celebration, when you are so full of joy that all
misery has left you, all darkness has left you. When you are so full that there is no emptiness in you,
that you have started feeling the signiﬁcance of the ordinary, day-to-day existence, when moment to
moment you live totally, intensely, passionately, then God is available.
God is not a person but just an experience, an experience of overwhelming mystery, unfathomable
mystery. It is not philosophy in the ordinary sense, it is not religion in the ordinary sense either. It is
philosophy in the truest sense of the word – philosophy means love for wisdom; then it is philosophy.
Religion, the word, very word, means to be in tune. It comes from RELIGERE: to be in deep harmony
with the whole, to be married with the whole, to be related with the whole, to forget your ego and
your separation. Then it is religion.
