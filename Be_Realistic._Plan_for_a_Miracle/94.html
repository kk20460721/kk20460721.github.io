CHAPTER
14
29 March 1976 pm in Chuang Tzu Auditorium
Arihanto means one who has attained, and anand means bliss – one who has attained bliss. and I
give you this name for a particular reason.
Start living from this moment as if you are blissful.
don’t try to achieve it – rather feel that it is
achieved, and now you have just to live it. Once you know that you have to live it, suddenly it starts
ﬂowing, mm?
Bliss is something which we bring with our birth. We have forgotten the language, so the language
has to be learned and this ’as if’ is the language.
[To someone who is leaving for the West:]
Mm, you can go – and help my work there. You can help many people
....
I will make you capable! You can be of much help. People never know their potentialities unless they
try.
I have never come across any human being who is ordinary. They may live ordinary... they may die
ordinary. They may never come to know what treasures they were carrying within – that’s another
thing. But I have never come across a human being who is simply ordinary. Everybody is unique and
extraordinary... and nobody actualises his potentialities. At the most we potentialise ﬁve percent.
Ninety-ﬁve percent of our treasures remain untouched.
So when I say you can, believe it. You can! If you think you can, you can. If you think you cannot,
you cannot.
