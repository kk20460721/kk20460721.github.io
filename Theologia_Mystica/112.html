18 August 1980 am in Buddha Hall
The ﬁrst question
Question 1
OSHO,
I UNDERSTAND YOU SAID THE OTHER DAY IN THE LECTURE THAT JESUS DID NOT WALK
ON WATER AND THERE ARE NO MIRACLES AS SUCH. BUT BY PRACTICING THE SAMYAMA
ON PATANJALI’S UDANA SUTRA, IS NOT MAN ABLE TO DO THIS?
PLEASE COMMENT.
Anand Guenter,
I SAY again that there are no miracles as such, because the whole existence is a miracle. What
more miracles can there be? Each moment, each event, is miraculous.
The religious person is one for whom everything, from the most ordinary to the most extraordinary,
has become a miracle. A seed growing green leaves, is it not a miracle far greater than any guy
waLking on water on the Sea of Galilee? A bird ﬂying in the sky, on the wing, is it not a greater
miracle than anybody walking in ﬁre? The roses, the lotuses, the marigolds, the millions of ﬂowers .
. . and you don’t see any miracles in them.
