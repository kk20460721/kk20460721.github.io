The heartbeat of the universe
30 May 1988 pm in Gautam the Buddha Auditorium
BELOVED OSHO,
A MONK SAID TO TOZAN, ”COLD AND HEAT DESCEND UPON US. HOW CAN WE AVOID
THEM?”
TOZAN SAID, ”WHY DON’T YOU GO WHERE THERE IS NO COLD OR HEAT?”
THE MONK SAID, ”WHERE IS THE PLACE WHERE THERE IS NO COLD OR HEAT?”
TOZAN SAID, ”WHEN COLD, LET IT BE SO COLD THAT IT KILLS YOU; WHEN HOT, LET IT BE
SO HOT THAT IT KILLS YOU.”
Maneesha, this is the very space that I am trying to help you to reach:
Where there is no cold, no heat, where there is only awareness, a silence that transcends duality,
cold and heat, day and night, life and death.
All that is dual is transcended when you are not in the mind but at your very center – which feels
neither cold nor hot, which is only a simple, pure space.
Tozan is speaking exactly about what you are doing every day.
