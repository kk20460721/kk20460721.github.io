The ﬁrst question:
Question 1
WHAT IS HERENOW?
DOES ’THOUGHT’ FORM PART OF IT?
IF SO, THEN ALL-TIME AND ALL-THINGS ARE NOW.
OR... IS HERENOW ONLY IN NO-MIND?
DIVYA, THOUGHT IS THE CAPACITY OF NOT BEING HERE – so thought cannot exist in the
herenow, it cannot be part of it. That is impossible. Thought can only be either of the past or of the
future. Thought can never be of the present. In the VERY process of thinking, that is implied; it is
intrinsic to it.
The moment you think, either you think of the past or you think of the future. It may be the immediate
past, but it is still past – it is never the present, it cannot be the present.
Thought needs space. And the present moment has no space in it. Thought creates the past and
the future to live in. The bigger the past, the more easily thought can move; the bigger the future,
again, the more easily thought can move. The present is not capable of giving that space for thought
to move.
