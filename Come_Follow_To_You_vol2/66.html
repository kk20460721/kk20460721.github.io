THOMAS CARLYLE ONCE SAID about one of his friends that he was born a
man and died a grocer. Everybody is born great and dies very small. Everybody
is born like a god and almost always dies like a dog. What happens in between?
Why is man crippled by life? One should hope that man grows to greater
dimensions, grows to greater heights, grows to greater life -- what Jesus calls'life
abundant' -- but that rarely happens. As it happens usually, man starts shrinking.
The moment man is born he starts shrinking, becoming smaller and smaller and
smaller. This has to be understood.
When a child is born he has no identity. He simply is. That'is-ness' is vast, it has
magnitude, it has no limitation. The child has no name yet, the name cripples; the
child has no identity, the child does not know who he is. That is his greatness,
that is his vastness. He is one with existence, he is not yet separated. He has no
boundary, no finitude.
