Every desire creates conﬂict
9 June 1987 pm in Chuang Tzu Auditorium
Question 1
BELOVED MASTER,
WHAT IS PEACE FOR THE REBELLIOUS MAN?
Raso, peace is, for the rebellious man, his very light. It is his very aroma, his fragrance, the harmony
of his heart, his at-oneness with existence. All conﬂicts of the mind are just memories of the past.
The mind is no longer divided, split or schizophrenic. The mind has become an organic unity.
The ordinary man who has not tasted rebellion or religion – which to me are equivalent – is a house
divided against himself. He is continuously ﬁghting within and without. He is ﬁghting for money, for
power, for prestige, for respectability. His outside life is nothing but power politics. It is a continuous,
ongoing warfare that ceases only when he stops breathing.
The inner scene is not much different either, because the outer and the inner cannot be much
different. They are part of one individual – the outside and the inside. Inside he is struggling against
nature – his own nature, his own instincts, which some so-called wise men have condemned. He
is blindly following their condemnation without any understanding of his own. Fighting with his own
nature, he becomes crippled.
