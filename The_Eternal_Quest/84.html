CHAPTER 9. THE NEED FOR AUTHENTICITY
Whatever Buddha or Jesus have said is not what they mean. To know the inner is one thing, but to
put it in words changes it completely. Jesus uses a different language than Buddha; it is bound to
be so. Buddha uses a different language because he is living in a different culture, a different world.
But whatsoever they know is the same. You can compare Buddha’s words with Jesus’. They are
different, the language is absolutely different. Jesus uses Jewish symbology. Buddha uses Hindu
symbology – they are different.
But if you have the same experience, then you can look beyond symbols and see that the
experiences of Buddha and Jesus are the same.
If you don’t have that inner experience, then
the languages will look different, contradictory.
A Christian scholar and a Buddhist scholar will never agree that the experiences of Jesus and
Buddha are the same. But the problem is semantic, one of language. Eckhart will agree that the
inner experience of the two is the same Jakob Bohme will agree, because they themselves have
achieved the same thing. Because they have achieved it, they can see beyond language. Language
communicates, but it also becomes a barrier.
