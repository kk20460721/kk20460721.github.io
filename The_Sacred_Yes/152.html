CHAPTER 30.
The work is not just work; it is a device.
If it was just work then there would not be much of a
question, whether one works or not is not much of a question, but it is a device too. It is part of your
inner growth. If somebody can surrender absolutely to the work, growth will happen in great strides.
And when you remain unoccupied your mind starts creating a thousand and one unnecessary
problems. To attain to a state where you can remain unoccupied and yet the mind will not create
problems is possible only at a later time. It happens, it bas to happen: one can sit for twenty-four
hours without doing a thing and there is no thought arising. But right now if you are sitting without
doing anything a thousand and one thoughts will arise – do this, do that – and the old mind will go
on churning and producing the same old patterns again and again.
