Maxims of Yoga: A sense of wonder
13 September 1974 am in Chuang Tzu Auditorium
[Note: This is a translation from the Hindi series Shiva Sutra, which is in the process of being edited.
It is for research only.]
A SENSE OF WONDER IS THE FOUNDATION OF YOGA. TO BE ESTABLISHED IN ONESELF
IS STRENGTH. A TRANSCENDENTAL LOGIC IS A MEANS TO SELF-REALIZATION. TO ENJOY
THE BLISSFULNESS OF THE EXISTENCE IS ENLIGHTENMENT.
A SENSE OF WONDER IS THE FOUNDATION OF YOGA.
Try to understand this.
In the dictionary, ”wonder” means astonishment. But there is a basic difference between wonder
and astonishment.
If you fail to understand this basic difference, you may embark on a different
journey altogether. Astonishment is the foundation of science, a sense of wonder is that of yoga.
Astonishment is extrovert; wonder is introvert. Astonishment is about the other; wonder is about our
own self. This is the ﬁrst point.
Wonder is born out of that which we cannot understand, which makes us speechless, which our
intellect cannot grasp, which proves to be bigger than us, before whom we are suddenly dumfounded
– which destroys us.
