A Silent Shrine
10 September 1977 am in Buddha Hall
THE SUFI SAGE, ABDULALIM OF FEZ, REFUSED TO TEACH, BUT FROM TIME TO TIME
WOULD ADVISE PEOPLE ABOUT THE WAY TO PROCEED ON THE PATH.
ONE DAY A DISCIPLE, WHO WAS BOTH INCAPABLE OF LEARNING AND REGULARLY DRIVEN
ABNORMAL BY ATTENDING ’MYSTICAL CEREMONIES’, VISITED HIM.
HE ASKED, ’HOW CAN I BEST PROFIT FROM THE TEACHINGS OF THE SAGES?’
THE SUFI SAID, ’I AM HAPPY TO BE ABLE TO TELL YOU THAT I HAVE AN INFALLIBLE METHOD
WHICH CORRESPONDS TO YOUR CAPACITY.’
’AND WHAT IS THAT, IF I AM ALLOWED TO HEAR IT?’
’SIMPLY STOP UP YOUR EARS AND THINK ABOUT RADISHES.’
’BEFORE, DURING OR AFTER THE LECTURES AND EXERCISES?’
’INSTEAD OF ATTENDING ANY OF THEM.’
MAN is a quest – not a question but a quest. A question can be solved intellectually, but a quest has
to be solved existentially. It is not that we are seeking some answers to some questions, it is that we
are seeking some answer to our being.
It is a quest because questions are about others. A quest is about oneself. Man is seeking himself.
