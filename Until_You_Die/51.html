CHAPTER 3. WALKING WITHOUT CRUTCHES
‘THIS IS NOT TRUE; MERELY A FANCY OF YOUR OWN,’ SAID THE CRIPPLES, BECAUSE BY
THAT TIME THEY WERE BECOMING BLIND AS WELL – BLIND BECAUSE THEY WOULD NOT
SEE.
If you want to see you have to die to your ego – because that ego won’t allow you to see, to be
natural, to allow the fact to emerge.
Die to the ego.
Die to the past.
And the Suﬁ saying is right: ‘You cannot have anything from me until you die.’
Die! It is hard, but it is the only way.
You cannot have anything from me until you die.
