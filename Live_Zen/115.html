Let no dust settle
3 May 1988 pm in Gautam the Buddha Auditorium
BELOVED OSHO,
SEPPO ADDRESSED THE ASSEMBLY AND SAID, ”ALL THE GREAT WORLD, IF I PICK IT UP
WITH MY FINGERTIPS, IS FOUND TO BE LIKE A GRAIN OF RICE. I THROW IT IN FRONT OF
YOUR FACE, BUT YOU DO NOT SEE IT. BEAT THE DRUM, TELLING THE MONKS TO COME
OUT TO WORK AND SEARCH FOR IT.”
SETCHO SAYS:
THE OX-HEAD DISAPPEARING, THE HORSE-HEAD APPEARS;
NO DUST ON THE MIRROR OF THE PATRIARCH SOKEI.
YOU BEAT THE DRUM AND SEARCH FOR IT IN VAIN.
FOR WHOM DO THE SPRING FLOWERS BLOOM?
OSHO,
WHY IS THE NATURE OF THE MIND SUCH THAT IT DOESN’T KNOW ITS OWN LIMITATIONS,
