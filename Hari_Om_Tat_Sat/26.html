Fear is to be understood, not conquered
19 January 1988 am in Gautam the Buddha Auditorium
Question 1
BELOVED MASTER,
I ALWAYS THOUGHT THAT ONE HAD TO CONQUER FEAR, AND THEN DEATH WOULD NOT
BE SCARY ANYMORE. THE OTHER DAY I WAS SITTING IN A PLACE IN THE HILLS WHERE
PEOPLE WERE KILLED AND I SAW THAT DEATH IS SWEET.
NOW I THINK IT MUST BE MORE LIKE THIS – THAT DEATH HELPS ONE TO UNDERSTAND
FEAR; THAT FIRST YOU EXPERIENCE DEATH AND SHE HELPS YOU SEE WHAT FEAR IS ALL
ABOUT. IS IT LIKE THIS? CAN YOU TALK ABOUT IT PLEASE?
Mukti Gandha, your question is based on complete misunderstanding, confusion. You will have to
understand ﬁrst where your confusion is. You say, ”I always thought that one had to conquer fear.”
This is the ﬁrst point of misunderstanding.
You cannot conquer fear. Fear has to be understood. The moment you start thinking of conquering
it, you have already accepted its existence, its power over you. And fear is just like a shadow: you
can ﬁght with it, but you cannot win. On the path one has to be very aware whether one is ﬁghting
with something that does not exist but is only his own projection; otherwise the journey goes on
becoming longer and longer.
Secondly, the language of conquering is not the language of a meditator, it is the language of
a soldier.
We have nothing to conquer.
