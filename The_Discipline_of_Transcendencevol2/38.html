Higgins lived in Staten Island, New York, and worked in Manhattan. He had to
take the ferry-boat home every night. One evening he got down to the ferry and
found there was a wait for the next boat, so he decided to stop at a nearby bar.
Before long, Higgins was feeling no pain.
When he got back to the ferry slip, the ferryboat was just eight feet from the
dock. Higgins, afraid of missing this one and being late for dinner, took a
running leap and landed right on the deck of the boat.
'How did you like that jump, buddy?' said Higgins to a deck-hand.
'It was great,' said the sailor. 'But why didn't you wait. We were just pulling in.'
