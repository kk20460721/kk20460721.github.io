CHAPTER
14
The Seed and The Tree
In Mt. Abu, Rajasthan, India
IT HAS BEEN POINTED OUT
THAT THE FRUITS OBTAINED BY THE
WORSHIP OF MANIFEST BRAHMAN
ARE DIFFERENT FROM THOSE OBTAINED
BY THE WORSHIP OF UNMANIFEST BRAHMAN.
WE HAVE HEARD THIS FROM THOSE WISE PEOPLE
WHO KNEW AND EXPLAINED IT TO US.
The Upanishads talk about two forms of Brahman, the supreme element. The forms are two, the
element is one. But it would be better to say there are two kinds of people who know, while the
element is one. The one is the unmanifest original form of Brahman; the other is the manifest form
of Brahman. The seed is the cause, the tree is the action, the effect. Everything is hidden in the
seed, everything is manifest in the tree. So there is one seed-Brahman, seen nowhere by us. What
is visible is the tree-Brahman, not the seed-Brahman. What has become manifest is seen by us; and
what is hidden, unmanifest, is invisible to us. Worship can be made of both unmanifest and manifest
Brahman, and in many and varied ways. The results of the two kinds of worship are quite distinct.
Worship of gods was very much in vogue when the Upanishads were sung.
