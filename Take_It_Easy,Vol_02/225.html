THE NEEDLESS BUDDHA?
THE ORIGINAL MAN IS THE GOAL OF BUDDHISM. What is meant by ’the original man’? The
original man is man without the mind, the original man is man without any conditioning. The original
man is the natural man.
We condition, we culture, we cultivate. We create certain types of minds – Hindu, Christian, Jain,
Buddhist. These are all the unnatural man. A Christian is an unnatural man, so is a Hindu. The
natural man cannot be Christian or Hindu. The natural man is simply a kind of is-ness, empti-ness
– with no ideology, with no character, with no morality, innocent, childlike.
THE ORIGINAL MAN
MUST RETURN TO HIS ORIGINAL PLACE...
And the way to return to that original place, to that original space, is awareness.
You have been conditioned.
You have been taught what to be, how to be, and you have been
following that conditioning automatically. You are living like a robot.
Buddha says: This robot-like existence has to be dropped. De-automatize it. And the only process
to de-automatize you is to become more alert. Do whatsoever you are doing with more awareness.
And you will be surprised that the more alert you are, the less rigid you become; the more alert you
are, the more unpredictable you become. The more alert you are, the more you function without
your habits. Otherwise you function just as habits, you go on functioning through habits.
When somebody asks a question, just wait for a moment before you answer. Just see for a moment
whether the answer is automatic. If it is automatic don’t give that answer, you are being a machine.
Respond. Somebody asks, ”Do you believe in God?” and an automatic answer comes, ”Yes, I am a
Catholic Christian.” Is this YOUR answer? or has this answer been given to you by others – by your
parents, teachers, church? If you were brought up in a Mohammedan country or if you were brought
up in a communist family, can you imagine the same answer?
