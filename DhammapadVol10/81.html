"Now look here, Jake," Moses protested. "We made our point. Now what do you say we
cut out the miracles and play a little golf?"
Buddha never did any miracle -- that is the greatest miracle. He is not interested in
mystifying you. His whole effort is to give you the key so you can open the doors of all
the mysteries. He is very existential, nonphilosophical, nonintellectual in his approach.
He is not heady -- but very practical, pragmatic. His whole approach is experimental,
experiential. So you will not be able to understand him if you only go on reading.
Try to experiment with what he is saying. Try to quieten your mind, reflect, watch, and
see yourself what happens: freedom, bliss, truth, wisdom, innocence, purity...
thousands of flowers start blooming in you. The spring suddenly bursts forth.
Enough for today.
