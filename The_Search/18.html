Dropping the Why
2 March 1976 am in Buddha Hall
The ﬁrst question:
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO, I DON’T KNOW WHY I AM HERE.
Nobody knows – and there is no way to know it, and there is no need to know it. This constant
questioning – why am I here?
why am I doing this?
– this constant hankering for the why, is a
disease of the mind. No answer is going to satisfy you, because the why can be asked again. If I
say something – you are here because of this – the why will be pushed back a little, that’s all. You
will again ask: Why? The why is nonending.
Once you understand it, you drop it. The why is ridiculous. Rather than asking: Why am I here? it
is better to use the opportunity, it is better to ﬂower, it is better to exist authentically. And this is the
beauty of it, that once you start existing authentically, truly, once you stop all nonsense thinking and
you start delighting in life, once you are no more a philosopher, the why is answered. But it is not
answered by anyone from the outside – it is answered by your own life energy.
The answer is possible, but it is not going to come like an answer, it is going to come like a lived
experience.
The answer is going to be existential, not intellectual.
The question is intellectual.
Drop it! Rather, be! Otherwise, you can go on asking
....
For centuries man has asked millions of
questions; not a single question has been solved by speculation, thinking, logic, reason. Not even
a single question is solved. On the contrary, whenever people have tried to answer a question, the
answer has created a thousand and one more questions.
