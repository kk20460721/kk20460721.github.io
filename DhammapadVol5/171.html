A small boy -- must have been six or seven -- was sitting on the steps of his house and
crying big tears.
An old man came by and he asked, "My son, why are you crying?" He wanted to help
the boy. He sat by his side, wiped his tears with his handkerchief and asked, "Why are
you crying? What has happened?"
The little boy said, "I am crying because I can't do what other boys are doing."
And the old man started crying!
The little boy was surprised. He said, "Pop, why are YOU crying?"
