The sky of completion
4 April 1989 pm in Gautam the Buddha Auditorium
BELOVED OSHO,
ONCE, HOTETSU – A DISCIPLE OF MA TZU – AND TANKA TENNEN, WERE ON A ZEN TOUR
VISITING VARIOUS ZEN MASTERS TO ASK QUESTIONS. ONE DAY, HOTETSU SAW FISH IN A
POND AND MOTIONED TO THEM WITH HIS HAND.
TANKA SAID, ”TENNEN.”
THE FOLLOWING DAY, HOTETSU ASKED TANKA, ”WHAT IS THE MEANING OF WHAT YOU
SAID YESTERDAY?”
TANKA THREW HIS BODY TO THE GROUND AND LAY THERE, FACE DOWN.
ON HIS LAST DAY, TANKA SAID TO HIS DISCIPLES, ”PREPARE A BATH FOR ME – I AM NOW
GOING.”
THEN HE PUT ON HIS STRAW HAT, HELD A STICK IN HIS HAND, PUT ON HIS SANDALS AND
TOOK A STEP FORWARD. BUT BEFORE HIS FOOT TOUCHED THE GROUND, HE HAD DIED.
