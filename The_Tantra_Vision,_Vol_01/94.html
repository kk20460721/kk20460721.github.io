CHAPTER 5. MAN IS A MYTH
THOUGH FROM A CLOUD ONE FEARS THE THUNDERCLAP...
He says: I am like a cloud and you are afraid because of the thunderclap, of the lightning.
But
remember:
THE CROPS RIPEN WHEN FROM IT POURS THE RAIN.
But if you allow me to rain on you, the seeds will sprout, sir, and the man that is still hidden
behind you, still not yet born, will be born; you will be able to ripen, to mature; you will be able
to blossom.
I invite you, says Saraha, for a great harvest...
the harvest of consciousness, the
harvest of awareness.
