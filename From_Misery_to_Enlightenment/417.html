One ma, one swami – and we can start the whole game again
24 February 1985 pm in Lao Tzu Grove
Question 1
OSHO,
IS THE WORLD GOING TO SURVIVE THE THIRD WORLD WAR?
I don’t have any hope, but I am hoping against hope.
There are many things to be considered.
First, perhaps it is good that it does not survive. It has become so rotten, so ugly, that if it survives
and remains the same it will be worse than not surviving.
Our world is in almost the same situation as thousands of people... around the world they are just
surviving because they can still breathe. In the hospitals, with all kinds of medical support, they can
continue to breathe; even the breathing is not their own – they need some mechanical device to help
them to continue to breathe.
Do you call that life? It is mere survival, not life – and mere survival is worse than death, because
death at least opens up a new door, cleanses the old, rotten stuff. That’s really the function of death:
it is a cleansing process.
