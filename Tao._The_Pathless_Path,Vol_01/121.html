CHAPTER 7. CHOOSE THE NEW
Always remember that when a new moment, a new insight, hovers around you, choose the new
because the old has not done anything for you, so what is the point of going back?
Even if the
new proves wrong, then too, choose the new. At least it will be a new adventure; you will come to
know something. Even if you don’t reach the goal, at least you will have learned some courage to
move into the unknown, to embrace the unknown. That will be your gain. But never choose the old.
Whenever there is a possibility for the new, go into the new. And go FAST because the old is very
heavy – it will pull you back.
Again and again I see a few people just sitting before me, hanging between their past and their
present.
Remember, present is very small and past is very long, so of course its weight is big.
Unless you are very courageous you will never get out of it. It is very comfortable and convenient to
remain with the past, but comfort and convenience are nothing. Growth is all.
Grow.
If growth comes through discomfort, inconvenience – good. Then inconvenience is good, beneﬁcial;
then discomfort is good, beneﬁcial. But always remember one thing: Go on growing. Don’t become
a rut. Don’t start moving in the same vicious circle again and again and again.
