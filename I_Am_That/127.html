CHAPTER 8. KNOWING NOTHING ABOUT EVERYTHING
The fourth member of the group, who had been listening intently, said nothing. He was the rabbi of
the village. The others expected him to reveal something, but he shrugged his shoulders as is the
way of all the Jews of all the ages. He simply shrugged his shoulders.
”Well,” asked one of them, ”Rabbi, how about you?”
”I would rather not confess,” he said shaking his head. They looked at him with suspicion. What
could he be covering up? Each of them accused him of being a poor sport, especially since they
had all opened up.
Finally the rabbi gave in. ”All right,” he said reluctantly, ”if you must know my weakness, it is scandal
– and I can hardly wait to get out of this place!”
