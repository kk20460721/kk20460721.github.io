30
The Taste of No-Mind
18 September 1986 pm in
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO,
EACH TIME I COME CLOSE TO YOU, IT LOOKS LIKE MY MIND DOESN’T WORK ANY MORE.
I CANNOT HOLD ON TO ANY CONCRETE THOUGHT; EVERYTHING DISAPPEARS AS IF IN A
WHITE, LIGHT CLOUD.
ON ONE SIDE IT IS LIKE COMING HOME AFTER ALL THIS INTENSE LONGING, AND ON THE
OTHER SIDE THE FEAR OF BECOMING CRAZY COMES UP.
IS THIS THE FEAR OF LOSING CONTROL, OR THE FIRST STEP OF BECOMING A DISCIPLE
AND PART OF A DIVINE MADNESS?
AM I ON THE RIGHT PATH?
Kavish, the mind is the wrong path, and the no-mind is the right path.
Mind is basically mad, and sanity is possible only, blossoms only, in a state of no-mind. If this is
remembered, then nothing else is needed.
Coming close to me, your mind is bound to disappear for the simple reason that I am not a mind.
The closer you come to me, the more you will be ﬁlled with a silence, serenity, no-mindness.
