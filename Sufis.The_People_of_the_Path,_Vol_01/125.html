CHAPTER 8. A CERTAIN MILIEU
She said, ’Yes. What happened to Katzie? Where did he go?’
Soen sa said, ’Where do you come from?’
’From my mother’s belly.’
’Where does your mother come from?’
She was silent.
Soen-sa said, ’Everything in the world comes from the same one thing. It is like in a cookie factory.
Many different kinds of cookies are made – lions, tigers, elephants, houses, people. They all have
different shapes and different names, but they are all made from the same dough and they all taste
the same. So all the different things that you see – a cat, a person, a tree, the sun, this ﬂoor – all
these things are really the same.’
’What are they?’
’People give them many different names. When you are thinking, all things have different names
and different shapes. But when you are not thinking, all things are the same. There are no words for
them. People make the words. A cat doesn’t say, ”I am a cat.” People say, ”This is a cat.” The sun
doesn’t say, ”My name is sun.” People say, ”This is the sun.” So when someone asks you, ’ What is
this?”, how should you answer?’
’I shouldn’t use words.’
Soen-sa said, ’Very good! You shouldn’t use words. So if someone asks you, ”What is Buddha?”
what would be a good answer?’
She was silent.
Soen-sa said, ’Now you ask me.’
’What is Buddha?’
Soen-sa hit the ﬂoor.
She laughed.
Soen sa said, ’Now I ask you, ”What is Buddha?”’
She hit the ﬂoor.
’What is God?’
She hit the ﬂoor.
’What is your mother?’
