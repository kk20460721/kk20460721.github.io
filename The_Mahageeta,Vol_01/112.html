Once Mulla Nasruddin boarded a bus. Absorbed in thought he took a seat and lit a cigarette.
”It is clearly written that smoking is forbidden on the bus,” the conductor angrily said. ”Didn’t you
read it? Don’t you know how to read?”
”I read it but there is so much written in the bus. Which of them should I do?” Nasruddin said. ”Look
at this: it says here, ’Always wear Handloom Saris!’”
One need only be a little careful to avoid such mistakes.
The body is very close – to read the
language of the body is very easy. And the body is so close that its shadow falls on the inner mirror
and is reﬂected. You are in the body, but you are not the body. The body is yours, you are not the
body’s. The body is your means, you are the end. Use the body, don’t lose your mastership. Though
living in the body, remain beyond the body – like a lotus in the water.
Hari Om Tat Sat.
