The Taste of Eternity
16 June 1980 am in Buddha Hall
The ﬁrst question:
Question 1
OSHO,
PLEASE EXPLAIN HOW I CAN MEDITATE OVER SOMETHING WITHOUT USING MY MIND.
DINESH,
Meditation has nothing to do with mind; meditation simply means a state of no-mind. The functioning
of the mind is the only disturbance in meditation. If you are trying to achieve meditation THROUGH
mind you are bound to fail, doomed to fail. You are trying to achieve the impossible.
A Zen initiate was meditating for years and whenever he would come to his Master, whatsoever
experience he would bring to the Master, the Master would simply reject: ”It is all nonsense. You go
back and meditate again.”
One day the Master came to the but of the disciple – he was sitting in a Buddha posture. The Master
shook him and told him, ”What are you doing here? If we needed stone Buddhas we have many in
the temple! Just by sitting like a stone Buddha you will not attain to meditation. Do what I have been
telling you to do. Just by stilling the body, your mind is not going to disappear, because it is through
the mind that you are enforcing a certain discipline on the body. Anything done by the mind is going
to strengthen the mind. It is a nourishment for the mind.”
