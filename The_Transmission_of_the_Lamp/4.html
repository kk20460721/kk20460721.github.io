CHAPTER 1. THE ANIMALS MUST ALL BE LAUGHING
Question 2
BELOVED OSHO,
I AM CONSTANTLY CRITICIZING MYSELF AND JUDGING OTHER PEOPLE. THIS MAKES ME
FEEL DIVIDED AND TENSE, AND I CANNOT ESTABLISH A REAL CONTACT WITH PEOPLE OR
NATURE. I WANT TO OPEN MY HEART AND I DON’T KNOW HOW TO DO IT.
PLEASE, COULD YOU SAY SOMETHING ABOUT THIS?
Our whole education is so ugly, our whole upbringing is so wrong, that it takes away every possibility
of your inner growth and gives you stupid ideas which have no relevance as far as your joy, your
understanding and your maturity are concerned.
Every child is being told, in thousands of ways, to criticize himself, so this is not only your problem.
He is always told he is wrong in everything.
Slowly, slowly he catches the disease of criticizing
himself.
And a person who criticizes himself cannot forgive others.
How can he forgive?
– for
the same reasons, he criticizes others. His whole life simply becomes a condemnation – condemn
yourself, condemn others. Then love becomes impossible, friendship becomes impossible, and he
simply suffers. And what he is criticizing is so absurd.
