6 February 1986 am in Kathmandu, Nepal
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO,
YOU DESCRIBE WITNESSING AS A KNACK. OFTEN, LATE AT NIGHT, WHEN I AM IN A VERY
RELAXED STATE, WITNESSING HAPPENS. AT OTHER TIMES, THOUGH, IT JUST SEEMS TO
BE MIND WATCHING MIND WATCHING MIND.
PLEASE COMMENT ON THIS KNACK.
The moment I say that witnessing is a knack, it implies that there is no way to explain it, no way to
teach someone about it, no way to train someone in it.
That’s the whole meaning of the word ”knack.”
I can say things which are close enough, but they can never be exact descriptions of the knack. It is
not an art, not a craft that can be explained in detail, step by step. But if it is happening to you, there
is no problem. You should know what it is, you should know the taste of it.
The problem is arising because you must be trying to do it; not allowing the knack to happen, but
trying to make an art of it, so that you can control it. Man wants to control everything; it is part of his
basic ego.
