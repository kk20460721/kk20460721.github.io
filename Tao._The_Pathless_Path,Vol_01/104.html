Choose the new
17 February 1977 am in Buddha Hall
TZU KUNG GREW WEARY OF STUDY AND TOLD CONFUCIUS ’I WANT TO FIND REST.’
’THERE IS NO REST FOR THE LIVING.’
’THEN SHALL I NEVER FIND IT?’
’YOU SHALL. LOOK FORWARD TO THE LOFTY AND DOMED MOUND OF YOUR TOMB AND
KNOW WHERE YOU SHALL FIND REST.’
’GREAT IS DEATH! THE GENTLEMAN FINDS REST IN IT, THE MEAN MAN SUBMITS TO IT!’
’TZU KUNG, YOU HAVE UNDERSTOOD. ALL MEN UNDERSTAND THE JOY OF BEING ALIVE,
BUT NOT ITS MISERY, THE WEARINESS OF GROWING OLD, BUT NOT ITS EASE, THE
UGLINESS OF DEATH, BUT NOT ITS REPOSE.’
PHILOSOPHY IS the enemy of truth.
And when I say philosophy I mean all philosophy, mine
included, because the philosophy creates a screen of words and you cannot see the reality as it
is. It distorts reality, it interprets reality, it gives a garb to reality, it hides reality, it covers reality.
Truth is naked, truth is all over, truth is within and without; and the only barriers are the words, the
theories, the theologies that you have learned. They don’t allow you to see that which is, they come
in the way, they are prejudices. All philosophy is a prejudice, and all concepts are not bridges – no
concept is a bridge – they are the barriers.
