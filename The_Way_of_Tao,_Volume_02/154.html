The signs of a saint: he is alert and irresolute, egoless and playful
17 April 1972 pm in Immortal Study Circle
THE SKILFUL MASTERS (OF THE TAO) IN OLD TIMES, WITH A SUBTLE AND EXQUISITE
PENETRATION, COMPREHENDED ITS MYSTERIES, WHICH WERE SO DEEP THAT THEY
ELUDED MAN’S KNOWLEDGE. AS THEY WERE THUS BEYOND MAN’S KNOWLEDGE, I WILL
MAKE AN EFFORT TO DESCRIBE OF WHAT THEY APPEARED TO BE.
CAUTIOUS, LIKE CROSSING A WINTRY STREAM.
IRRESOLUTE, LIKE ONE FEARING DANGER ALL AROUND.
GRAVE, LIKE ONE ACTING AS A GUEST.
SELF-EFFACING, LIKE ICE BEGINNING TO MELT.
UNPRETENTIOUS, LIKE WOOD THAT HAS NOT BEEN CARVED.
VACANT, LIKE A VALLEY.
AND DULL LIKE MUDDY WATER.
This is a rare sutra. It is an uncommon sutra because it is entirely opposite to our concept of a saint.
Lao Tzu does not look at a saint as we do. Lao Tzu’s saint is a more integrated individual, more
complete. The one whom we look upon asa saint is an imperfect individual. It would be better to
call him a good man rather than a saint. We can understand a good man and an evil man. He who
