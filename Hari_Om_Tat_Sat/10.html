Nobody is at home
18 January 1988 am in Gautam the Buddha Auditorium
Question 1
BELOVED MASTER,
MY GIRLFRIEND TOLD ME I AM A LITTLE BORING, NOT VERY JUICY, VERY DEPENDENT AND
A VICTIM. THEN I FELT VERY GUILTY AND DEPRESSED AND UTTERLY UNWORTHY. I BEGAN
TO FEEL INSIDE ME A BIG NO: TOWARDS EXISTENCE, LIFE, LOVE, YOU. MEANWHILE I
OBSERVED IN ME THIS DESTRUCTIVE ENERGY AND I FELT THAT I SOMEHOW ENJOYED
IT!
BELOVED MASTER, IS IT POSSIBLE TO USE THIS ENERGY IN SOME CREATIVE WAY?
Anand Veetkam, your question is an example of the stupid conclusions that mind comes to. Perhaps
you may not have looked into it and its contradictions. I would like to go into the very psychology of
such questions. They are not only within you, they are within many. You are courageous to expose
yourself.
The exposure begins, ”My girlfriend told me I am a little boring.” Your girlfriend is very compassionate,
because each man ﬁnally becomes very boring, not a little boring. Do you realize the fact that what
you call love is a repetition, the same stupid gymnastics again and again? And in this whole stupid
game the man is the loser.
He is dissipating his energy, perspiring, hufﬁng, pufﬁng, and the girl
keeps her eyes closed, thinking, ”It is a question only of two or three minutes and this nightmare will
be ﬁnished.”
