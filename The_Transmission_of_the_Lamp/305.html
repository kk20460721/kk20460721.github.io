Question 2
BELOVED OSHO,
WATCHING THE BREATH IS MY MEDITATION. I FIND IT MIRACULOUS. IS IT A METHOD THAT
NEEDS TO BE DROPPED, AND IF SO, DOES IT DROP ON ITS OWN?
WOULD YOU SPEAK MORE ABOUT VIPASSANA MEDITATION?
Kaveesha, there is nothing more to say about vipassana meditation. The word ‘vipassana’ means
watching, particularly watching the breath – as it comes out, as it goes in. You simply continue to
watch it, its movement in and out.
And the method has not to be dropped, because when the time comes it disappears of its own
accord. When your watchfulness is perfect, the method disappears. All the methods that I have
given to you are such that you will not need to drop them.
Just use them to perfection, and the
moment they are perfect they will drop on their own – just like ripe fruit falling from the tree. And
when a method disappears on its own, it has a beauty; then your watchfulness is unscratched.
You are on the right path; just continue till the method disappears of its own accord, and you are left
simply a watcher on the hill.
