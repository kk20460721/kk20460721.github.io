CHAPTER 1. BE UNPREDICTABLE
I have heard the story of a very great surgeon, a world-famous surgeon, who was retiring. He was
seventy-ﬁve; still no young man was capable of competing with him – at the age of seventy-ﬁve his
hands were like steel.
He was retiring, and all his friends were there: there was great dancing and joy and eating and
drinking. But he was sitting in a corner, sad. Somebody said to him, ”This is not a time to be sad.
Everybody is enjoying – why don’t you come and enjoy?”
He said, ”This is a time for me to be sad. I never wanted to be a surgeon. My whole life I wasted
being a surgeon.
Although I became the topmost surgeon in the world, it has not given me any
contentment. I wanted to be a dancer, and even if I had been a street dancer, it would have been
closer to my heart’s content.”
So let the people be what they are. Help them to be what they want. Never impose. And this is
respect for humanity.
