CHAPTER 8. CLEAN THE PATH, REMOVE THE ROCKS
Eleven-year-old Lucy was walking down the village street leading a cow by a rope. She met the
vicar who said, ”Little girl, what are you doing with that cow?”
”Please Sir,” said Lucy, ”it is my father’s cow and I am taking her to the bull.”
”Disgusting,” said the parson, ”can’t your father do that?”
”No,” said Lucy, ”it has to be the bull.”
Everybody has his own purpose, that’s right, but to ﬁnd your purpose is impossible without ﬁnding
yourself. And the moment you ﬁnd yourself, simultaneously you will ﬁnd your purpose. So there is
no need to be concerned with the purpose. The whole concern should be knowing yourself, and the
way to know yourself is meditation.
