Straight To Freedom
14 April 1975 am in Buddha Hall
A
MAN
CAME
TO
THE
GREAT
TEACHER
BAHAUDIN
AND
ASKED
FOR
HELP
IN
HIS
PROBLEMS, AND GUIDANCE ON THE PATH OF THE TEACHING.
BAHAUDIN TOLD HIM TO ABANDON SPIRITUAL STUDIES AND LEAVE HIS COURT AT ONCE.
A KIND-HEARTED VISITOR BEGAN TO REMONSTRATE WITH BAHAUDIN.
‘YOU SHALL HAVE A DEMONSTRATION,’ SAID THE SAGE.
AT THAT MOMENT A BIRD FLEW INTO THE ROOM, DARTING HITHER AND THITHER, NOT
KNOWING WHERE TO GO IN ORDER TO ESCAPE.
THE SUFI WAITED UNTIL THE BIRD SETTLED NEAR THE ONLY OPEN WINDOW OF THE
CHAMBER, AND THEN SUDDENLY CLAPPED HIS HANDS.
ALARMED, THE BIRD FLEW STRAIGHT THROUGH THE OPENING OF THE WINDOW, TO
FREEDOM.
THEN BAHAUDIN SAID:
‘TO HIM THAT SOUND MUST HAVE BEEN SOMETHING OF A SHOCK, EVEN AN AFFRONT, DO
YOU NOT AGREE?’
