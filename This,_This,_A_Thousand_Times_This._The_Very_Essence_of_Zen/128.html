Audrey and Marilyn, two retired schoolteachers from Chicago, save up all their money to go on safari
in Africa.
They are having a wonderful time going through the jungle, when suddenly a huge gorilla swings
down out of the trees, sweeps Marilyn into his arms, and disappears.
He takes her back to his cave, and for a week makes love to her all day and night.
Finally, Audrey organizes a rescue party, and Marilyn is saved and rushed to the hospital.
She is treated there for a couple of days and then her friend comes for a visit.
”Oh, Marilyn!” the friend sobs, ”what a ghastly experience! How do you feel?”
”How should I feel?” sobs Marilyn, ”he never writes, he never calls...!”
Now, the ﬁrst step is gibberish. Say anything, relevant, irrelevant; don’t bother, because nobody is
listening. Only those who are listening are idiots.
Speak Chinese if you don’t know it, speak Japanese if you don’t know it, but don’t speak the
language you know. Just go crazy. Once in a while it is such a tremendous relief to go crazy.
