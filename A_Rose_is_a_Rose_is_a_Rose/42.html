Never believe anything unless you have experienced it.
Never form any prejudice, even if the whole world is saying that it is so, unless you have encountered
it.
In India there has been a great mystic, Kabir. He says, ’Never believe in the ears – just believe in
the eyes. All that you have heard is false. All that you have seen is true.’
This should be carried as a constant remembrance because we are human beings and we tend to
say falacies. We are part of this whole mad world, and that madness is inside every human being.
It has not to be allowed to overpower you. One has to remember continuously. And if this much can
be done
....
It is arduous, because prejudices are very comfortable and easy; you don’t have to pay
for them. Truth is costly, precious. You have to pay much. In fact you have to put your whole life at
stake. Then you arrive at it. But only truth liberates.
So looking at other people and the functioning of their mind, always remember that the same type
of mind is hidden in you also. So never listen to it. It will persuade you; it will argue, it will try to
convince you. Just tell it, ’I will see for myself. I am still alive. I can encounter whatever is needed.’
That is the difference between belief and trust. Belief Is through the mind. Trust is through your own
experience. Belief is just intellectual. Trust is total.
