WHAT IS A SELFISH DESIRE? In the Buddhist way of expression, a selfish
desire is one that is based in the self. Ordinarily, in ordinary language, we call a
desire selfish if it is against somebody else and you don't care about others. Even
if it harms others, you go ahead and you fulfil your desire. People call you selfish
because you don't care for others, you don't have any consideration for others.
But when Buddha says a desire is selfish, his meaning is totally different. He
says: If a desire is based in the idea of self then it is selfish.
For example: you donate, a million rupees you donate, for some good cause --
hospitals to be made or schools to be opened, or food to be distributed to the
poor, or medicine to be sent to poor parts of the country -- nobody will call it a
selfish desire. Buddha will say it is -- IF there is any motivation of self. If you are
thinking that by donating a million rupees you are going to earn some virtue and
