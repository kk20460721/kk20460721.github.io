It is that fateful night in old Jerusalem town. Upstairs at the downtown MacDonald’s burger house,
Jesus is host at his famous farewell dinner party.
Ronald MacDonald, the clown, is running around giving everyone party hats and whistles, trying to
get the serious saints to laugh.
But Jesus has been looking moody all night when suddenly, he puts down his wineglass and shouts
across the smoke-ﬁlled room, ”Peter!” he cries, pointing his ﬁnger. ”Peter! Tonight, you are going to
betray me!”
”Ah no, Lord!” calls back Peter, turning pale, ”I would never betray you!”
”Okay, Peter,” says Jesus, ”I was just checking up on you. Come over here and eat some bread and
drink some wine with me.”
So they eat bread and drink some wine. And then Jesus says, ”Okay, Peter, you can go.”
Then Jesus looks around the room again, and calls John, his beloved disciple.
”John!” shouts Jesus. ”John! Tonight you are going to betray me!”
”Ah no, Lord!” replies John, falling off his seat in shock. ”I would never betray you. It is just not
possible!”
