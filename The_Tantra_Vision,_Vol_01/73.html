Question 4
OSHO, I LAUGH SO MUCH AT YOUR JOKES. I WOULD LIKE TO ASK ONE QUESTION: WHY
DOES A JOKE CREATE SO MUCH LAUGHTER?
ONE THING: you have never been allowed to laugh; your laughter is repressed. It is like a repressed
spring – any excuse is enough and it comes out. You have been taught to be sad, long faces; you
have been taught to be serious.
If you are serious, nobody thinks that you are doing something wrong; it is accepted, it is the
way things have to be. But if you are laughing, laughing too much, then people will start feeling
embarrassed by you. They will start thinking something is weird:’Why is this man laughing’?” And if
you are laughing without any reason, then you are mad; then they will take you to the psychiatrist;
then they will hospitalize you. They will say, ”He laughs without any reason! Only mad people laugh
without any reason.”
In a better world, in a more civilized world, in a really civilized world, laughter will be accepted as
natural. Only when a person is sad will we hospitalize him.
Sadness is illness:
laughter is health.
So, because you have not been allowed to laugh.
