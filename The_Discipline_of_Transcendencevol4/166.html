Another Hassid story I would like to share with you
....
A woman came to Rabbi Israel, the Magid of Kosnitz, and told him with many
tears that she had been married a dozen years and still had not borne a son.
"What are you willing to do about it?" he asked her.
She did not know what to say.
"My mother, " so the Magid told her, "was aging and still had no child. Then she
heard that the holy Baal Shem was stopping over in Ept in the course of a
journey. She hurried to his inn and bade him pray that she might bear a son.
"'What are you willing to do about it?' Baal Shem asked her.
"'My husband is a poor bookbinder,' she replied, 'but I have one fine thing that I
shall give to the Rabbi.'
"She went home as fast as she could and fetched her good cap, her katinka,
which was carefully stored away in a chest. But when she returned to the inn
with it, she heard that Baal Shem had already left for Magditch.
"She immediately set out after him, and since she had no money to ride, she
walked from town to town with her katinka, until she came to Magditch.
"The Baal Shem took the cap and hung it on the wall. 'It is well,' he said. "My
mother walked all the way back from town to town until she reached Ept. A year
later, I was born."
"I too," cried the woman, "will bring you a good cap of mine so that I may get a
son."
"That won't work," said the Magid. "You heard the story; my mother had no
story to go by."

life is unrepeatable. You can read the VEDAS and the KORAN and the BIBLE: it
won't help. It may help the RISHI who wrote the VEDA, but it won't help you --
because it is a borrowed thing to you. The rishi had never read any VEDA,
Mohammed had never read any KORAN. YOU cannot have that which
Mohammed gained, because he had never read any KORAN. And you are trying
to gain it by reading the KORAN? No, it is not possible.
