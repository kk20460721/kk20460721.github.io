CHAPTER 15.
[A sannyasin, arriving, says: I want to have deeper relationships with people.]
The very expectation creates trouble. If you want deeper relationships you will never be natural. The
hankering for the deeper will disturb your spontaneity. You will be trying and making every effort to
make it deep. and it cannot be made deep by effort; efforts keep everything shallow.
You have to drop the very desire for deepening it. Enjoy it as it is, and then it deepens – not by
desiring but by enjoying. So whatsoever is available in the moment, enjoy it! Even though it is not
deeper, what is wrong in a shallow relationship?
It has its own beauty, it has its own freedom. A deeper relationship has its own problems, remember.
One becomes more entangled, and to escape becomes more and more difﬁcult; with a shallow
relationship you can always escape!
So enjoy – when it is shallow, swim; and when it is deep, dive!
