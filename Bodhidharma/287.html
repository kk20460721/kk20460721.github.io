always means no-mind. When the mind is extinguished, the same energy that
was involved in the mind becomes your meditation.

I have told you that in English there is no one word which can translate DHYAN.
There are three words: the first is concentration, which is the lowest. It means
focusing your mind on one thing or one thought. It is useful in science; in fact
without concentration there would be no science at all. Science is the by-product
of concentration.
The second word in English is contemplation, which is higher than
concentration. Contemplation means thinking about a certain subject matter --
not a single thought, but a stream of thoughts confined to the same subject
