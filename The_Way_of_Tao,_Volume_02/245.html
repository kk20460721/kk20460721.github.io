The birth of doctrines from the decline of Tao
18 June 1972 pm in Immortal Study Circle
ON THE DECLINE OF THE GREAT TAO,
THE DOCTRINES OF HUMANITY AND JUSTICE AROSE.
WHEN KNOWLEDGE AND CLEVERNESS APPEARED,
GREAT HYPOCRISY FOLLOWED IN ITS WAKE.
WHEN THE SIX RELATIONSHIPS NO LONGER LIVED AT PEACE,
THERE WAS (PRAISE OF) ’KIND PARENTS’ AND ’FILIAL SONS’.
WHEN A COUNTRY FELL INTO CHAOS AND MISRULE,
THERE WAS PRAISE OF ’LOYAL MINISTERS’!
This sutra deals with the most difﬁcult aspect of life. Lao Tzu is opposed to everything that we look
upon as a great ethical doctrine. For his basic doctrine is that a profound balance is constantly being
established each moment in life. If we concentrate on goodness, badness increases. If we stress
morality, immorality develops in the same ratio. If we wish that people should be good, we shall only
succeed in creating more bad people. If we try to understand life, we shall come to one conclusion:
that life is impossible without a balance. And this balance is everywhere, in all directions.
Scientists have, of late, developed a unique conception.
This conception can ﬁll us with anxiety
but not Lao Tzu.
