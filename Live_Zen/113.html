CHAPTER 11. WHAT’S IN A NAME?
Hymie Goldberg is on a business trip and ﬁnds himself having to use a paytoilet in a public building.
He is making himself comfortable, when he notices that the toilet paper has run out.
Seeing a pair of shoes in the next booth, he calls out, ”Excuse me, friend, but do you have any toilet
paper in there?”
”No,” comes the reply, ”I am afraid there is not any here either.”
Hymie pauses for a moment. ”Listen,” he says, ”do you happen to have a newspaper or a magazine
with you?”
”Sorry,” comes the reply, ”I don’t.”
Hymie pauses again.
”Well,” he says, ”how about Ronald Reagan’s postal stamp?”

tired and worn out, so I brought her home and made her a sandwich.

they went out of fashion.

the color does not suit you.
”Her jeans were also worn out, so I gave her a pair of yours, that were perfectly good until you gained

use any more?’”
