COURAGE IS THE BEST DESTROYER: COURAGE ALSO DESTROYS PITY. PITY, HOWEVER, IS
THE DEEPEST ABYSS: AS DEEPLY AS MAN LOOKS INTO LIFE, SO DEEPLY DOES HE LOOK
ALSO INTO SUFFERING.
COURAGE, HOWEVER, IS THE BEST DESTROYER, COURAGE THAT ATTACKS: IT DESTROYS
EVEN DEATH, FOR IT SAYS: ‘WAS THAT LIFE? WELL THEN! ONCE MORE!’
I am reminded of a small anecdote. In the Soviet Union, in the middle of the night, a KGB man
knocks on a door and shouts, ”Is Ginsberg inside?”
Somebody opens the door, and the KGB man says, ”I am from the KGB. Is Ginsberg inside?”
The man said, ”Ginsberg? He is dead.”
The KGB man said, ”Dead? Who are you? What is your name?”
The man said, ”My name? My name is Ginsberg.”
The KGB man said, ”Are you insane or something? Just now you said that Ginsberg is dead.”
The man laughed and he said, ”Do you call this living? Even in the middle of the night one cannot
sleep peacefully – do you call this living?”
If, at the time of death, death inquires of you, ”Would you like to live your life – the same life that you
have lived – one time more?” what do you think your answer is going to be? I don’t think that any
intelligent man would be ready to live this whole tragedy again – exactly the same wife, the same
husband; exactly the same drama, the same dialogues.
But a man who has lived life not as a misery, but as an exploration into new experiences – always
moving, always going upward; always searching for something better, always creating himself;
always destroying all that is rubbish in him and bringing out the best – perhaps he may say, ”Well
then, once more – no harm!”
But only a man who has really lived intensively and totally and who has not been lukewarm and
tepid, who has burnt his life’s torch at both ends together, will be ready to go through life again
