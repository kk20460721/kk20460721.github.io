The great love affair with the universe
3 December 1987 am in Chuang Tzu Auditorium
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO,
I LOVE TO CRY AND TO FEEL THIS SWEET PAIN OF LONGING IN MY HEART THAT HAS BEEN
VISITING ME FOR A WHILE. IT SEEMS TO BE A TREASURE THAT IS BRINGING ME BACK
AFTER HAVING BEEN LOST IN THE MIND. I HAVE EXPERIENCED A STILLNESS BEYOND
THIS PAIN AND TEARS; YET, SOMETIMES IT FEELS AS IF I WOULD LOVE TO CRY MY WAY
TO GOD.
BELOVED,
BEAUTIFUL
MASTER,
IS
IT
POSSIBLE
TO
INDULGE
TOO
MUCH
IN
THIS
SENSATION IN MY HEART?
Prem Udgita, there are a thousand and one ways to reach to the ultimate. If your crying is out of joy,
then your tears are more valuable than any laughter can be. It all depends on the quality. Tears can
be of pain, tears can be of blissfulness, tears can be of silence, tears can be of gratitude. And when
tears are of gratitude it is not suffering, it is rejoicing – just in your own style.
I don’t deny any possibility of reaching to the divine.
And all possibilities are available.
Just
choose the one that suits you best. Don’t be concerned and don’t compare with others; that brings
complexity. Just watch yourself. If your tears are helping your growth, making you richer, making
you more loving, making you more lively, if you feel tears are bringing your spring closer, these same
tears will turn into ﬂowers. So you have to be careful.
