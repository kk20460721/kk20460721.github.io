1
16 January 1976 pm in Chuang Tzu Auditorium
[A sannyasin says: I’m changing all the time... Since the group, I don’t know... I don’t like talking, I
don’t like laughing... I feel like crying... ]
Whatsoever happens, accept and enjoy, and don’t force anything.
If you feel like talking, talk.
If
you feel like being silent, be silent – just move with the feeling. Don’t force in any way, not even for
a single moment, because once you force anything you are divided in two – and that creates the
problem, then your whole life becomes split.
The whole of humanity has become almost schizophrenic, because we have been taught to force
things. the part that wants to laugh and the part that doesn’t allow you to laugh become separate,
and then you are divided in two. You create a topdog and an underdog, and so there is conﬂict. The
rift that the conﬂict creates can go on becoming bigger and bigger and bigger. So the problem is
how to bridge that rift, and how not to create it anymore.
In Zen they have a very beautiful saying. They say,
Sitting,
just sit,
walking,
just walk...
above all,
don’t wobble.
