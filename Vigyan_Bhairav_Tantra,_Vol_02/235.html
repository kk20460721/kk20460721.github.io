CHAPTER
18
In your hands
29 June 1973 pm in Bombay, India
Question 1
TECHNIQUES ARE SHORTCUTS, REVOLUTIONS, BUT ARE NOT THESE AGAINST TAO,
SWABHAV, THE NATURE?
They are.
They are against tao, they are against swabhav.
Any effort is against shabhav, tao;
effort as such is against tao. If you can leave everything to swabhav, tao, nature, then no technique
is needed, because that is the ultimate technique.
If you can leave everything to tao, that is the
deepest surrender possible. You are surrendering yourself, your future, your possibilities. You are
surrendering time itself, all effort. This means inﬁnite patience, awaiting.
If you can surrender everything to nature then there is no effort, then you don’t do anything. You just
ﬂoat. You are in a deep let-go. Things happen to you, but you are not making any effort for them –
you are not even seeking them. If they happen, it is okay; if they don’t happen, it is okay – you have
no choice. Whatsoever happens, happens; you have no expectations and of course, no frustrations.
Life ﬂows by, you ﬂow in it. You have no goal to reach, because with the goal effort enters. You have
nowhere to go, because if you have somewhere to go, effort will come in; it is implied. You have
nowhere to go, nowhere to reach, no goal, no ideal; nothing is to be achieved – you surrender all.
In this surrendering moment, in this very moment, all will happen to you.
Effort will take time;
surrender will not take time. Technique will take time; surrender will not take time. That’s why I call it
the ultimate technique. It is a no-technique. You cannot practise it – you cannot practise surrender.
