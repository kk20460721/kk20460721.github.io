2 November 1971 pm in Immortal Study Circle
CHAPTER 5: SUTRA 1
NATURE
HEAVEN AND EARTH DO NOT ACT FROM (THE IMPULSE OF) ANY WISH TO BE BENEVOLENT;
THEY DEAL WITH ALL THINGS AS THE DOGS OF GRASS ARE DEALT WITH. THE SAGE DOES
NOT ACT FROM (ANY WISH TO BE) BENEVOLENT; THEY DEAL WITH THE PEOPLE AS THE
DOGS OF GRASS ARE DEALT WITH.
From all the people who have known on this earth, Lao Tzu is matchless, he is unique.
A man of ordinary intellect can easily add any of his own ideas to Krishna’s GITA. In the same
manner, anything can be added to the words of Mahavira, Buddha or Christ; and it will be difﬁcult to
detect these. The statements of these saints do not fall contrary to the morals and understanding
of the common man. This is why all the scriptures of the world have become interpolated. Each
generation adds something new of its own. Thus it has not been possible to preserve the purity of
these scriptures.
But Lao Tzu’s book is one of those very few books on earth which has preserved its purity. Nothing
can be added unto it. The reason is, only a person of the calibre of Lao Tzu can ever add anything to
it. The things Lao Tzu talks of are so contrary and opposed to the general common understanding,
that an ordinary man cannot add anything of his own. To add anything to his works, a man has to
become like Lao Tzu.
