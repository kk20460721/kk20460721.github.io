"Who are you?" If you have heard the question, you know the direction. And the
answer can only be: "Return back. Follow that direction, move back."
But your questions are false. You have not heard them; somebody else has taught
them to you. Your questions are false, and then your answer becomes false. You
learn the question from others, you learn the answer from others, and you
remain phoney.
The word 'phoney' comes from 'telephone'. Have you ever observed that
listening to a man directly -- listening to your wife or husband or friend directly,
face-to-face -- is a reality because personal contact is there, but listening to him
on the phone everything becomes phoney? One never knows who is there --
whether the person is really there or if it is a tape recorder, a dictaphone. Nobody
knows. And the sound seems to be coming not from the heart, but from the
mechanism.
Have you sometimes watched people...? I have watched a few people, I know a
few people, who will put their specs on whenever they phone. I was worried:
"What is the matter? The person is not there, so who are you trying to see?" Just a
deep urge to see the person. A deep urge -- otherwise the thing seems to be
phoney. But this is how the mind has become corrupted.
I went with a friend once to visit the Taj Mahal. He is a good photographer -- and
he had no time to see the Taj Mahal, he was seeing it through the camera lens. I
told him, "We have come here to see the Taj Mahal."
He said, "Forget about it. It is so beautiful that I will take pictures and see them at
home! " But those pictures are available everywhere -- what is the need to come
to the Taj Mahal? The direct vision is lost.
Mulla Nasrudin's first child was born. I went to see him The Mulla was sitting
with the boy, a small boy -- beautiful. I said, "How beautiful! "
He said, "This is nothing -- you should see the photagraphs!"
