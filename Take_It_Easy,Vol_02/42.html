HOW CAN THERE BE ANY GENERALIZATIONS ABOUT THE QUALITIES OF MAN AND
WOMAN?
Generalizations are not possible about ANYTHING, because no individual will ﬁt them.
But still,
generalizations are meaningful; they simply indicate. When I say a woman is more concerned with
the immediate, I am not saying anything about a particular woman – Deborah, or anybody else. I am
simply saying it about WOMANNESS, that womanness is concerned with the immediate. And if you
are not concerned with the immediate then something somewhere in your womanhood is missing.
That is very essential to femininity: the concern for the immediate, the imminent.
But generalizations are generalizations, remember it.
And there will be differences between
individuals. But the meaning of a generalization is just to indicate a certain quality. It doesn’t say
anything about particular individuals, it simply says the quality of being a woman is immediateness.
