CHAPTER 1. JOKES OF MULLA NASRUDDIN
”IT AIN’T SAFE UP THERE,” said Nasrudin. ”NO DRIVER.”
26.
Mulla Nasrudin and his wife were arguing.
”I was a fool when I married you,” said the wife.
”I GUESS YOU WERE,” replied Nasrudin, ”BUT I WAS SO INFATUATED AT THE TIME, I DIDN’T
NOTICE IT.”
27.
The town’s richest man had died. The next morning, another rich, and particularly miserly, old man
said to Mulla Nasrudin, ”I wonder how much he left.”
Mulla Nasrudin laughed and said, ”EVERY CENT OF IT, SIR.”
28.
Mulla Nasrudin used to say:
”Every man should have at least one wife, because there are
somethings that just can’t be blamed on the government.”
29.
Mulla Nasrudin had just checked into the hotel.
”Welcome,” said the clerk at the desk. ”We want you to know you are welcome. We are going to do
everything we can to make you comfortable and help you to feel at home.”
”PLEASE DON’T,” said the Mulla. ”I LEFT HOME SO I COULD FIND A CHANGE. FOR THE NEXT
FEW DAYS I WANT TO FEEL AS IF I AM AT A BEACH RESORT.”
30.
The lady said to Mulla Nasrudin at the door, ”Have you ever been offered work?”
”Only once Lady,” said Nasrudin. ”Aside from that, I HAVE MET WITH NOTHING BUT KINDNESS.”
31.
The judge was questioning Mulla Nasrudin. ”I understand that your wife is scared to death of you,”
he said.
”That’s right, your Honor,” said the Mulla.
The judge leaned over and whispered in his ear, ”Man to man,” he said, ”HOW DO YOU DO IT?”
