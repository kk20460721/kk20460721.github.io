And he went on a pilgrimage to Mecca. Why? Later on, when he was asked he said, ”Just to absorb,
just to digest what that madman had given me so suddenly. It was too much! It was overﬂowing, I
was overwhelmed; it had to be digested. He had given me more than I was worthy of.”
So he went to Mecca on a pilgrimage, to meditate, to be silent, to be a pilgrim unknown to anybody,
to be anonymous. The thing had happened, but it had to be absorbed. The light had happened, but
one has to get accustomed to it.
And when he became accustomed to the new gestalt, to the new vision, he came back to Lai-Khur
and presented him this book, THE HADIQA. That’s what he wrote on the way back from Mecca.
He poured his experience, his satori, into this book. These words are saturated with satori. This
is how this great book was born, like a child is born, mysteriously; like a seed becomes a sprout,
mysteriously; like a bird comes out of the egg, mysteriously. Like a bud opens early in the morning
and becomes a ﬂower, and the fragrance is spread to the winds.
Yes, this book was not written. This book is a gift from God. This book is a gift from God, and a
gratitude from Hakim Sanai to that strange madman, Lai-Khur.

NO OBSTACLE REMAINED.
HAKIM SANAI WAS A MAN OF CHARACTER, a religious man. He had tried hard, he had tried all
the possible ways to reach God. And he was a very intelligent man, knowledgeable, and was known
as a wise man. He was a very capable, rational person. He had tried in every possible way to reach
God through reason.
But nobody has ever reached God through reason. That is not the door to him, that is the wall that
prevents you. Reason is perfectly capable of knowing the superﬁcial, but it cannot dive deep into the
depths. It knows only how to swim on the surface. Reason is perfectly good as far as the journey
OUTWARDS is concerned, but it is utterly impotent as far as the journey inwards is concerned.
Reason is good and adequate if you want to know about matter. But it is utterly incapable if you want
to know anything about consciousness. Reason can measure, but consciousness is immeasurable.
Reason can weigh, but consciousness has no weight.
