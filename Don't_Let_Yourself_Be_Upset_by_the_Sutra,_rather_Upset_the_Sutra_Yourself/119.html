25 August 1979 pm in Chuang Tzu Auditorium
Deva Louis. Deva means divine; louis means glory – divine glory. It is a seed in us, hence we are
unaware of it. It is yet a potential, that’s why we are oblivious of it. It has to be realized. It is a journey
to be made; it is a promise to be fulﬁlled.
Man is not what he appears; he is far more, inﬁnitely more. If you believe only in that which you are,
then life is meaningless, then it is trivial, then it consists only of the very ordinary, the routine, the
repetitive, the mechanical. Then it is ugly and it seems to be a very cruel joke. God seems to be
absolutely absent; the devil seems to be very much present.
If man is what he appears to be – if that’s all – then it is really some wicked energy that is behind
existence. Then the whole thing is a mockery. But if we start growing, if we start transforming the
potential into the actual, if we start becoming a plant, a ﬂower, a fragrance, then life has beauty,
tremendous beauty. Then life is an ecstatic pilgrimage and each moment brings more and more
perfection.
Each moment brings you closer and closer to God and each moment becomes a
surprise, a great gift.
Unless one starts growing, one never becomes aware of what we are meant to be. We are part of
God and part of his glory and part of his beauty and part of his eternity. We are not less than God:
at those heights, at those altitudes, the part is equal to the whole.
In lower mathematics the part is never equal to the whole. How can the part be equal to the whole?
That is the world of logic – where a part is a part and the whole is the whole, and the whole is
bigger than the part.
But as you penetrate into the mysterious, as you go deeper and deeper
into your inﬁnity, a moment comes when lower mathematics does not function any more; a higher
mathematics starts functioning.
