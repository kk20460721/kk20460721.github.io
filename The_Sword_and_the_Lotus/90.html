CHAPTER
9
One boat is enough
28 January 1986 pm in
Question 1
BELOVED MASTER,
CAN A MAN START A JOURNEY IN TWO BOATS – REJOICING THE WORLD, AND CHEERING
GOD?
Man has been doing that since the very beginning, and he is still doing it. He is riding in two boats,
and the result is the misery, the suffering, the anguish all over the world. You cannot ride in two
boats.
Firstly, being in two boats you become split, you become two – you are divided. A house divided
against itself cannot stand long, and a man divided against himself is simply sick.
The psychoanalysts call that sickness schizophrenia: he is not one. He has two voices – always in
conﬂict with himself. He wants to do one thing, but half of his being pulls him back: ”Don’t do it.”
If he wants to do something else, the other half pulls him back and says, ”Don’t do that.” Caught
between the conﬂict of his own inner rift, all man’s energy is wasted. He ﬁnds himself in a constant
nightmare.
You are all doing it, because all your religions have been teaching you to do it – not clearly, not
openly, but in a very subtle way.
