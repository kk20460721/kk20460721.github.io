Following more than fifty years of atheism, scientists in Russia began to be curious
about what religion might be. A group of them took a book of holy quotations and
decided to have it decoded by an analogical computer. They opened the book and took
the first phrase they saw, typing it out onto the keyboard. The phrase was: "The spirit is
willing, but the flesh is weak." They crowded around the printout as the words began to
appear.
As they read the message their astonishment increased: "The vodka is ready, but the
meat is devitalized."
"No wonder religions used to mystify people," they muttered to one another.
Then one of them had an idea. He tapped out the book's title, UNCONSIDERED
TRIFLES, onto the decoder. Out came the translation: "Neglected puddings."
"You see!" he shouted. "You have got the wrong book -- this is one about the abuses of
cookery."
