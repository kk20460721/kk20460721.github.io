Spreading a net of perfume
28 May 1986 am in Punta Del Este, Uruguay.
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO,
WHENEVER YOU TALK ABOUT MUSIC AND DANCE, IT TOUCHES SOMETHING REALLY DEEP
IN ME, AND MY WHOLE BEING IS ABSORBED IN EVERY WORD AND GESTURE, LIKE A
SPONGE. WHEN YOU TALKED ABOUT AWARENESS AND BEING TOTALLY LOST IN THE
MUSIC, I SUDDENLY UNDERSTOOD WHAT AN INCREDIBLE GIFT YOU HAVE GIVEN ME BY
LETTING ME DANCE AROUND YOU – WHERE NOTHING WAS SAID, BUT AWARENESS HAD
TO BE THERE, AND IT CAME NATURALLY, TOGETHER WITH BEING LOST IN THE DANCE.
IN MOMENTS LIKE THAT I AM FILLED WITH AWE AND GRATEFULNESS, JUST SEEING HOW
YOU GUIDE US SO PERFECTLY AND SO CARINGLY AND HOW YOU CREATE SITUATIONS
THROUGH WHICH, WITH JUST A LITTLE BIT OF EFFORT, WE CAN GAIN SO MUCH.
I AM SO IMMENSELY GRATEFUL TO BE HERE WITH YOU.
Gayan, the most important thing in spiritual guidance is that it should not be direct.
The guided
should not feel he is being guided.
Whenever guidance is direct it brings a kind of slavery; the
guided becomes dependent on the guide.
But to guide indirectly is very difﬁcult. It is a kind of persuasion where the guided cannot feel at all
that he is being told to do this, to do that. He is simply being given indications, with such love, that he
likes to go in those directions in those moments. And he becomes aware that he has been guided
on each step – carefully guided – but only when he has reached.
