Jainism says: It will happen by our will, our power. There is no grace, no help
coming from the universe, so we have to fight and struggle. So Jainism becomes
a very egoistic trip,
Buddha says: You have to do all that you can do. Be a Jaina first, and then be a
Hindu. That is the greatest synthesis ever brought to the world: be a Jaina first,
and then be a H indu.
In Buddha the whole east came to a synthesis. Buddha's contribution to the
world is unique. And that's my teaching too: be a Jaina first, and then be a
Hindu. First make all the effort that you can, go to the very end that you can, and
then relax. Then you have earned; now the grace will be coming.
Effort plus surrender, struggle plus surrender, brings one home.
