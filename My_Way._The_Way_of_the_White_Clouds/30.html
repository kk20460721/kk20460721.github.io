CHAPTER
4
All Hopes are False
13 May 1974 am in Buddha Hall
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO,
YOU HAVE BEEN TELLING US HOW EASY IT IS TO DROP OUR EGOS AND BECOME ONE
WITH THE WHITE CLOUD.
YOU HAVE ALSO TOLD US THAT WE HAVE HAD MILLIONS OF LIVES AND MANY OF THEM
HAVE BEEN WITH BUDDHAS, KRISHNAS AND CHRISTS – YET STILL WE HAVE NOT GIVEN
UP OUR EGOS.
AREN’T YOU CREATING FALSE HOPES IN US?
All hopes are false. To hope is to be false. So it is not a question of creating false hopes: whatsoever
you can hope will be false. Hope comes out of your falsity of being. If you are real there is no need
for any hope. Then you never think about the future, about what is going to happen. You are so real,
so authentic that the future disappears.
When you are unreal then the future becomes very signiﬁcant, then you live in the future. Then your
reality is not here and now, your reality is somewhere in your dreams. And you make those dreams
look real because through those dreams you gain your reality. As you are, you are unreal. That’s
why so much hoping goes on. All hopes are false; you are real. My whole effort is how to throw you
to yourself.
