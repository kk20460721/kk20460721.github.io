To be Miserable or Ecstatic...?
12 May 1974 am in Buddha Hall
Question 1
BELOVED OSHO,
YOU ONCE TOLD US A STORY ABOUT AN OLD MAN WHO WAS OVER ONE HUNDRED YEARS
OLD. ONE DAY, ON HIS BIRTHDAY, HE WAS ASKED WHY IT WAS THAT HE WAS ALWAYS
HAPPY. HE REPLIED: EVERY MORNING WHEN I WAKE UP I HAVE THE CHOICE TO BE HAPPY
OR UNHAPPY, AND I CHOOSE TO BE HAPPY.
HOW IS IT THAT WE USUALLY CHOOSE TO BE UNHAPPY? HOW IS IT THAT WE DON’T FEEL
AWARE OF THE CHOICE?
This is one of the most complex human problems. It has to be considered very deeply, and it is
not theoretical – it concerns you.This is how everybody is behaving – always choosing the wrong,
always choosing the sad, the depressed, the miserable. There must be profound reasons for it, and
there are.
First thing: the way human beings are brought up plays a very deﬁnite role in it. If you are unhappy
you gain something from it, you always gain. If you are happy you always lose.
From the very beginning an alert child starts feeling the distinction.
Whenever he is unhappy
everybody is sympathetic towards him, he gains sympathy. Everybody tries to be loving towards
