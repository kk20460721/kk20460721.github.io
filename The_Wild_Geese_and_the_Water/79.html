CHAPTER 6. STILL BELIEVING IN LEPRECHAUNS?
”Look ’ said Peckham, ”I will give you a chance to get even. Three thousand six hundred dollars or
nothing.”
Rabbi Goldstein agreed and lost again.
Later in the locker room, Rabbi Goldstein said, ”You like to wager. I will bet you one thousand dollars
I can pee over your head from ten feet.”
”All right,” said the club prexy. ”But nobody can pee that far.”
Rabbi Goldstein proceeded to pee all over Peckham.
”See,” he said, after changing his clothes. ”I knew you could not do it. Come on, I will buy.”
At the bar they met Lockwood, the local bank chairman. ”This is Rabbi Goldstein,” said the club
president. ”He is one of our new members.”
Peckham then told Lockwood about the money he had won, including how Rabbi Goldstein had
peed all over him.
”Oh no!” cried the banker, ”That s.o.b. bet me twenty thousand dollars he would piss all over the
president on his ﬁrst day at the club!”
