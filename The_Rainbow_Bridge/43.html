CHAPTER 9.
Man lives in constant discontent, discontent about everything. That discontent keeps you in a state
of storm, in a state of fever, chronic fever. Because of it you cannot know who you are: so much
noise inside, so much turmoil. You cannot Ô¨Ånd the way to your innermost core: there is so much
smoke.
One has to learn to become a little more contented. One has to learn to accept things as they are.
One has to learn, not to complain, but to accept, and not only to accept, but accept gratefully. The
mind of discontent asks for that which is not and never is thankful for that which is. And the being of
contentment is just the opposite; it never asks for that which is not and thanks for all that which is.
With this shift of consciousness from discontent to contentment the storm starts subsiding and the
smoke starts disappearing and the noise is no more so much. And slowly slowly a stillness settles
in.
Then you can hear the still small voice within you, then you can see who you are. And in that seeing
you see God; in that seeing you see the whole secret of existence.
